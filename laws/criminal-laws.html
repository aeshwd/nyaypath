Criminal law in India deals with offenses against the state or society. It includes punishments for crimes such as **murder, theft, fraud, assault, and cybercrimes**.

üìú Major Criminal Laws in India

  • Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860: Defines crimes and punishments.
  • Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC), 1973: Procedures for investigation and trial.
  • Indian Evidence Act, 1872: Governs admissibility of evidence in court.
  • Juvenile Justice Act, 2015: Laws related to minors in crime.
  • Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act, 1985: Covers drug-related crimes.

üî¥ Types of Criminal Offenses

  • Cognizable & Non-Cognizable: Serious offenses (murder, rape) require immediate police action, while minor ones (defamation, trespassing) need court approval.
  • Bailable & Non-Bailable: Minor offenses allow bail (theft), but serious ones require court approval (attempt to murder).

üèõÔ∏è Criminal Case Procedure

  1. Filing of FIR: The police register the complaint.
  2. Investigation: Collection of evidence, statements.
  3. Arrest & Bail: Accused may be arrested or granted bail.
  4. Trial: Presentation of arguments and evidence in court.
  5. Judgment: The court pronounces its decision.
  6. Appeal: The accused can challenge the judgment in a higher court.

üìú Landmark Criminal Cases

  • Kasab Case (2012): Ajmal Kasab was sentenced to death for the 26/11 Mumbai attacks.
  • Nirbhaya Case (2012): Led to stricter rape laws in India.
  • Jessica Lal Murder Case (2010): Ensured justice despite initial acquittals.

üìú Legal Rights of Accused

  • Right to Silence: An accused person cannot be forced to confess.
  • Right to Legal Aid: Free legal aid must be provided.
  • Right to Fair Trial: Every accused has the right to a fair trial.
  • Right Against Double Jeopardy: Cannot be punished twice for the same crime.

⚖️ Civil Laws in India

Civil laws in India regulate **non-criminal disputes** such as contracts, property, family issues, and torts. They govern individual rights and obligations.

📜 Major Civil Laws in India

  • Contract Law: Governs agreements between parties.
  • Property Law: Deals with land ownership, transfer, and inheritance.
  • Family Law: Includes marriage, divorce, child custody, and succession.
  • Consumer Law: Protects consumers from unfair trade practices.
  • Tort Law: Covers personal injuries, defamation, and negligence cases.

📜 Important Acts & Sections

  • Indian Contract Act, 1872: Defines contracts, breach, and enforcement.
  • Transfer of Property Act, 1882: Covers property sales, gifts, and leases.
  • Hindu Succession Act, 1956: Governs inheritance in Hindu families.
  • Specific Relief Act, 1963: Deals with remedies for contract violations.
  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019: Protects buyers from fraud and misleading advertisements.

⚖️ Civil Case Procedure in India

A civil case follows these steps:

  1. Filing of Suit: The plaintiff files a complaint in court.
  2. Issuance of Summons: Defendant is notified to appear in court.
  3. Written Statement: The defendant responds to the claims.
  4. Trial: Both parties present their evidence.
  5. Judgment & Decree: The court issues its final ruling.
  6. Appeal: Either party can appeal the decision in a higher court.

🏛️ Important Case Laws

  • Balfour v. Balfour (1919): Distinguished social agreements from enforceable contracts.
  • M.C. Mehta v. Union of India (1986): Strengthened environmental law and liability.
  • Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India (1978): Expanded the scope of fundamental rights.

📜 Legal Remedies in Civil Law

  • Compensatory Damages: Financial compensation for loss.
  • Injunction: A court order to stop or enforce an action.
  • Specific Performance: Court enforces a contract obligation.

Cyber laws in India regulate **internet usage, data protection, and digital security**. These laws help prevent cybercrime and ensure safe digital transactions.

üìú Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000

The **IT Act, 2000** is the primary legislation governing cyber laws in India. Key provisions:

  • Section 43: Data protection & hacking penalties.
  • Section 66: Hacking & identity theft (punishment: up to 3 years in jail).
  • Section 66A: Punishment for offensive messages sent online (struck down in 2015).
  • Section 67: Publishing obscene material online (punishment: up to 5 years in jail).
  • Section 72: Breach of privacy & confidentiality.

üíª Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use **strong passwords** and enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)**.
  • Be cautious of **phishing emails and scam links**.
  • Do not **share sensitive data** on unverified websites.
  • Report cybercrime at **cybercrime.gov.in**.

üìú Legal Remedies for Cybercrime Victims

  • File a **FIR at a local police station** or cyber cell.
  • Report financial frauds to **RBI & CERT-In**.
  • Complain to **social media platforms** for online abuse.

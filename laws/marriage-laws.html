💍 Marriage Laws in India

Marriage laws in India are governed by **religious customs** and **statutory laws**. The primary laws include:

📜 Major Marriage Laws in India

  • Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 – Applies to Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs.
  • Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937 – Governs Muslim marriages.
  • Christian Marriage Act, 1872 – Governs marriages among Christians.
  • Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act, 1936 – Applicable to Parsis.
  • Special Marriage Act, 1954 – For inter-religious and civil marriages.

🔹 Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

The Hindu Marriage Act applies to Hindus, Jains, Buddhists, and Sikhs. Key provisions:

  • Section 5: Conditions for a valid marriage (age, consent, monogamy, etc.).
  • Section 7: Ceremonies of marriage.
  • Section 13: Grounds for divorce (adultery, cruelty, desertion, etc.).

🔹 Muslim Marriage Law

A Muslim marriage (Nikah) is a **contract** under **Shariat Law**, requiring:

  • Consent: Both parties must consent.
  • Mahr: A mandatory gift from the groom to the bride.
  • Iddat Period: A waiting period before remarriage after divorce.
  • Talaq: Various forms of divorce (Talaq-e-Ahsan, Talaq-e-Hasan, Triple Talaq).

🔹 Christian Marriage Act, 1872

The Christian Marriage Act requires:

  • Church Marriage: Conducted by a priest.
  • Marriage Registration: Mandatory for legal recognition.

🔹 Special Marriage Act, 1954

The **Special Marriage Act** allows inter-religious and civil marriages.

  • 30-day Notice: Marriage officers must publish a notice.
  • Both parties must be unmarried at the time of marriage.

💔 Divorce Laws in India

Divorce laws vary by religion. Common grounds include:

  • Adultery: A spouse having extra-marital relations.
  • Desertion: Abandonment for at least two years.
  • Mental & Physical Cruelty: Abuse, harassment, or harm.
  • Mutual Consent Divorce: If both partners agree.

🏛️ Important Case Laws

  • Shah Bano Case (1985): Supreme Court ruled that a divorced Muslim woman can claim maintenance under **Section 125 CrPC**.
  • Lata Singh v. State of U.P. (2006): Upheld right to inter-caste marriage.
  • Joseph Shine v. Union of India (2018): **Adultery decriminalized** under IPC Section 497.

📜 Marriage Registration Process

Marriage registration is required under **Hindu Marriage Act** or **Special Marriage Act**:

  • Apply at the local **Marriage Registrar’s Office**.
  • Submit proof of age, address, and passport-size photos.
  • Two witnesses are required.

📜 Rights of Spouses

  • **Right to Maintenance:** Wife can claim financial support.
  • **Right to Matrimonial Property:** Equal share in assets.
  • **Right to Protection from Domestic Violence:** Under Domestic Violence Act, 2005.
